About me

PhD - Plant breeding, genetics, and biotechnology
Michigan State University

Area of Expertise: Quantitative trait loci analysis, genome-wide association and genomic prediction in dry bean.

I completed my bachelor’s degree in biology at the University of Tolima in Colombia. After getting a bachelor’s degree, I worked at the International Center of Tropical Agriculture (CIAT) in dry beans and then at the Colombian Sugarcane Research Center (CENICAÑA). I have worked on genotype-phenotype association in several traits of agronomic importance. I received my Ph.D. in Plant breeding and genetics from Michigan State University working on the implementation of genomic prediction in dry beans to increase the genetic gain per unit of time in traits such as yield, mineral concentration, and end-use quality traits. Currently, I am a research associate at the Great Lakes Bioenergy Research Center and my research focuses on computational sciences and genomic prediction in several crops, including switchgrass, dry bean and corn.
