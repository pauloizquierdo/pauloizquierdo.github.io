Research

Within my research path, I have worked in universities and research centers which have provided me with valuable opportunities to learn about plant breeding in different crops such as dry beans, sugarcane, and cowpea. These experiences have been instrumental in learning to work efficiently in field and laboratory settings, as well as for developing bioinformatics and data analysis skills. At MSU, I have gained plant breeding understanding through classwork and my own research and research collaborations, and I have learned to work collaboratively with colleagues from diverse research groups in the United States, Canada, Colombia, Uganda, and Zambia whom have different cultures, academic backgrounds, and goals. Read about my current and past work below.

﻿QTL analysis of cooking time

﻿Cooking time is a major factor associated with consumer preference of dry beans. In this study we genotyped a biparental population that was planted in Africa and United State, and we identify three QTL that reduce the cooking time and increase the protein content in seed.

Genome-Wide Association for agronomic traits

A MAGIC population was used to uncover the genetic basis governing the phenotypic responses to drought in dry bean. A vast amount of genotypic and phenotypic data was generated allowing the identification of candidate genes for further use un plant breeding.

Meta-QTL analysis of seed Fe and Zn concentration

Dry bean is an important source of microelements in human diet. In this study we conducted a meta-analysis from seven populations to identify genomic regions that control Fe and Zn concentration in seed. The meta-qtl regions identified in this study could be used to generate markers for marker-assisted selection.

Genomic Selection for end-use quality

We used genomic selection for end-use quality traits cooking time in a diversity panel of dry bean. The prediction accuracies were high on the extreme phenotypes which indicate that genomic selection is a powerful tool to facilitate germplasm selection for high concentration of zinc and protein in grain and to reduce the cooking time.
